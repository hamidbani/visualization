      Visualization: Our World in Data – CO₂ Emissions by Country Over Time
      Why I classified this as a good visualization:
      I chose this visualization because it presents complex information in a clear, honest, and accessible way. It uses a line graph to show CO₂ emissions by country over time, which is an effective choice because it helps you see trends and changes over decades.The graph is clean and interactive—you can hover over different lines to see specific values, or choose which countries you want to focus on.       Bad Visualization
      Fox News Pie Chart on Job Creation by U.S. Presidents

      This pie chart is a good example of how a simple design choice can mislead viewers. The biggest issue is that the chart’s slices add up to more than 100%, which completely defeats the purpose of using a pie chart. Pie charts are supposed to show parts of a whole, so when the total is over 100%, it’s not just confusing—it’s incorrect.
      On top of that, the colors used in the chart don’t make much sense, and there’s no clear explanation or source for the numbers. The chart doesn’t say which years are being measured, or whether the data comes from the same time period for each president. There’s no title or legend either, so the viewer is left guessing about what the chart is really saying.
      From a design perspective, it’s also visually overwhelming. The slices are poorly spaced, and the proportions don’t seem accurate at all. It’s hard to trust what you’re seeing, and that’s a big red flag for any data visualization.
